--- title: "Germany (2015, 2016, 2017, 2018)" excerpt: "Beautiful Nature, Skeptical People
" collection: photography --- I visited Germany multiple times for solo hiking trips, with friends, and also with my wife. However, on each trip I experienced racism in some or other form (e.g., someone showing me the middle finger), or when I asked for an English menu I was told in German that perhaps it will suit me better if I ask for one in Arabic. I decided to stop visiting Germany despite having many German friends, and the only exception was made to visit Munich and Berlin because my wife really wanted to see them. Overall, I do not intend to visit Germany again for hiking purposes.

Trails

Multiple trails in Garmisch Partenkirchen, Mittenwald, Eifel national park, and Berchtesgaden.

Food

Germany has its own local food and beer culture and I found that fascinating. Contrary to what people may think there are many different types of local cuisine in Germany. Although the food is simple, it is definitely the better one among all Germanic countries and worth an experience. Local German breads with Ragi and local German beer are probably among the best I have had in Europe.

Transportation

I never tried hitchhiking in Germany. Public transport is expensive but high quality and reliable.

People

I have had multiple first hand experiences of facing racism and also have had many friendly experiences. I accepted both, because this is how the world is, and one cannot travel only expecting positive experiences everywhere. Having said that, I am grateful to all the German people on couchsurfing who shared their house and food with me.

Special Experiences

Sauna at my couchsurfing host's place in Mittenwald, and the view of Berchtesgaden valley from a mountain top with the moonlight being reflected from snowy peaks and the cloud inversion below getting illuminated by lights from the houses.
